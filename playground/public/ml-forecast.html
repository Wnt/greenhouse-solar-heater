How the ML Forecast Works

The experimental machine-learning engine behind the Next 48 h card — a random forest that learns thermal physics, rolled forward one hour at a time.

The big idea

Three things to know first

🌲

Not a time-series model

The random forest predicts a single 1-hour temperature change from the conditions of that hour — each step stands on its own, the way Nord Pool price models work.

🔁

Rolled forward 48×

A full forecast is built by feeding the model's own output back in as the next hour's input — a recursive rollout. That's why error grows with horizon.

🔀

Mode is the bridge

The controller logic decides when to heat; the forest only learns how temperature responds in each mode. Your tuning thresholds act on the first half.

Why it's built this way

What the formulation buys us

The forest learns physics, not a calendar.

By predicting a per-hour change from instantaneous conditions, the same model works at any hour of any day — it never memorises a time series.

The controller logic stays separate.

Step 1 is pure hysteresis from control-logic.js. Step 3 is the learned thermal response. Your tuning thresholds change the forecast through step 1 at inference time — which is why they don't need to be model features.

Errors compound — honestly.

Because each hour's prediction feeds the next, small mistakes accumulate. The card reports a confidence level and drops to low when conditions stray outside the model's trained range.
